Sharia Compliance Framework

Not just halal
in name. In practice.

We do not use the word "Sharia-compliant" lightly. This page exists to show our work — every standard we hold ourselves to, grounded in Quran, Sunnah, and the principles of Islamic business ethics (fiqh al-muamalat).

An honest note: There is no formal "halal certification" body for marketing agencies. Rather than claim a certificate that doesn't exist, we do something more Islamic — we declare our standards publicly, anchor them in daleel, and hold ourselves accountable before Allah and the community. If we fall short, we want to be told.

01
First Principle
Sidq
الصِّدْق
Truthfulness in all things
Quran — Surah Al-Baqarah 2:283
وَلَا تَكْتُمُوا الشَّهَادَةَ ۚ وَمَن يَكْتُمْهَا فَإِنَّهُ آثِمٌ قَلْبُهُ
"And do not conceal testimony, for whoever conceals it — his heart is indeed sinful."
Surah Al-Baqarah (2:283)
Hadith — Sahih Bukhari
عَلَيْكُمْ بِالصِّدْقِ فَإِنَّ الصِّدْقَ يَهْدِي إِلَى الْبِرِّ
"You must be truthful, for truthfulness leads to righteousness, and righteousness leads to Paradise."
Sahih al-Bukhari 6094, Sahih Muslim 2607

In marketing, sidq means every claim we make for a client must be true. No inflated results. No fake reviews. No misleading before-and-after comparisons. No false urgency ("Only 2 left!" when there are 200). If we cannot honestly represent a product or service, we will not market it.

How we apply Sidq
  • All ad copy is reviewed for truthfulness before publishing
  • We report honest performance metrics — good or bad
  • Testimonials used only with genuine client consent and accuracy
  • We tell clients what we cannot deliver — before signing, not after
What we refuse to do
  • ✕
    Write false or exaggerated ad copy
  • ✕
    Create fake urgency or scarcity claims
  • ✕
    Produce or solicit fake reviews
  • ✕
    Conceal fees, deliverables, or performance issues from clients
02
Second Principle
Amanah
الأمانة
Trust & Integrity
Quran — Surah Al-Anfal 8:27
يَا أَيُّهَا الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا لَا تَخُونُوا اللَّهَ وَالرَّسُولَ وَتَخُونُوا أَمَانَاتِكُمْ
"O you who have believed, do not betray Allah and His Messenger or betray your trusts while you know."
Surah Al-Anfal (8:27)
Hadith — Sahih Bukhari & Muslim
آيَةُ الْمُنَافِقِ ثَلَاثٌ: إِذَا حَدَّثَ كَذَبَ، وَإِذَا وَعَدَ أَخْلَفَ، وَإِذَا اؤْتُمِنَ خَانَ
"The signs of a hypocrite are three: when he speaks he lies, when he makes a promise he breaks it, and when he is entrusted he betrays."
Sahih al-Bukhari 33, Sahih Muslim 59

When a Muslim business owner hands us their brand, their budget, and their reputation — they have placed an amanah in our hands. That trust is sacred. We treat every client's money as if it were our own, and every campaign as if our akhira depended on its integrity.

How we apply Amanah
  • Client budgets are managed with full transparency and detailed accounting
  • We keep every promise made in our agreements
  • Client data and business information is never shared or exploited
  • We honor deadlines or communicate delays proactively
What we refuse to do
  • ✕
    Overbill or pad ad spend for agency profit
  • ✕
    Share client strategies or data with competitors
  • ✕
    Make promises in a proposal we cannot keep in delivery
03
Third Principle
Halal Only
الحلال فقط
Permissible clients & content
Quran — Surah Al-Baqarah 2:275
وَأَحَلَّ اللَّهُ الْبَيْعَ وَحَرَّمَ الرِّبَا
"Allah has permitted trading and forbidden interest."
Surah Al-Baqarah (2:275)
Quran — Surah Al-Ma'idah 5:2
وَتَعَاوَنُوا عَلَى الْبِرِّ وَالتَّقْوَىٰ ۖ وَلَا تَعَاوَنُوا عَلَى الْإِثْمِ وَالْعُدْوَانِ
"And cooperate in righteousness and piety, but do not cooperate in sin and aggression."
Surah Al-Ma'idah (5:2)

This ayah is the foundation of our entire client policy. Marketing a haram business makes us a cooperator in that sin. We refuse all clients whose core business is impermissible — regardless of how much revenue it costs us. This is non-negotiable.

Industries we serve
  • Halal restaurants, food producers, and halal butchers
  • Islamic schools, masjids, and educational institutions
  • Modest fashion and Islamic products
  • Muslim-owned professional services (law, real estate, healthcare)
  • General halal consumer goods and home services
Industries we will never serve
  • ✕
    Alcohol, nightclubs, and entertainment with haram content
  • ✕
    Gambling, casinos, and betting platforms
  • ✕
    Conventional interest-based financial products (riba)
  • ✕
    Pork products and non-halal meat
  • ✕
    Adult content, dating apps, or anything immodest
  • ✕
    Tobacco and vaping products
04
Fourth Principle
No Gharar
لا غرر
No deception or uncertainty
Hadith — Sahih Muslim
نَهَى رَسُولُ اللَّهِ صَلَّى اللَّهُ عَلَيْهِ وَسَلَّمَ عَنْ بَيْعِ الْغَرَرِ
"The Messenger of Allah (peace be upon him) forbade transactions involving gharar (deception/uncertainty)."
Sahih Muslim 1513

Gharar in marketing takes many forms: hidden fees, vague contract terms, manipulative psychological tactics, dark patterns in UX, or deliberately obscuring a product's limitations. Any tactic designed to confuse, pressure, or mislead a consumer is gharar — and we don't use it.

How we eliminate Gharar
  • All service agreements are written in plain, clear language
  • No dark patterns, hidden checkboxes, or auto-renewing traps in campaigns we build
  • Pricing is stated clearly — no surprise charges after onboarding
  • We disclose when a result is uncertain rather than guaranteeing what we can't
Tactics we refuse to use
  • ✕
    Countdown timers on offers that don't actually expire
  • ✕
    Bait-and-switch pricing or hidden retainer clauses
  • ✕
    Manipulative psychological pressure tactics in copy
  • ✕
    Misleading "free" offers with concealed conditions
05
Fifth Principle
No Riba
لا ربا
Interest-free dealings
Quran — Surah Al-Baqarah 2:278-279
يَا أَيُّهَا الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا اتَّقُوا اللَّهَ وَذَرُوا مَا بَقِيَ مِنَ الرِّبَا إِن كُنتُم مُّؤْمِنِينَ
"O you who believe, fear Allah and give up what remains of riba, if you are truly believers."
Surah Al-Baqarah (2:278)

Our service contracts are structured as fixed-fee or retainer agreements — never as interest-accruing arrangements. We do not charge late fees compounded with interest. We do not use payment processors that embed riba structures. Where a client faces hardship, we prefer to renegotiate terms rather than penalize with interest charges.

Our financial practices
  • Flat monthly retainers — no interest-based payment structures
  • Late payment handled through renegotiation, not compounding fees
  • We do not market riba-based financial products for any client
  • Halal payment alternatives offered where available
06
Sixth Principle
Ihsan
الإحسان
Excellence as worship
Hadith — Sahih Muslim
إِنَّ اللَّهَ كَتَبَ الإِحْسَانَ عَلَى كُلِّ شَيْءٍ
"Indeed Allah has prescribed ihsan (excellence) in all things."
Sahih Muslim 1955
Hadith — Bayhaqi
إِنَّ اللَّهَ يُحِبُّ إِذَا عَمِلَ أَحَدُكُمْ عَمَلاً أَنْ يُتْقِنَهُ
"Indeed Allah loves that when one of you does a deed, he does it with itqan (perfection and excellence)."
Shu'ab al-Iman by Al-Bayhaqi 5314, graded Hasan by Al-Albani in Sahih al-Jami 1880

Ihsan means doing everything as though Allah is watching — because He is. For us, mediocre work is not a halal option. Every campaign, every deliverable, every email to a client is an act of ibadah when performed with sincerity and excellence. We don't just meet the standard — we seek to exceed it, because that is what Allah loves.

What Ihsan looks like in our work
  • We do not submit work we are not proud of
  • Client success is treated as our own success
  • We pursue continuous improvement in knowledge and skill
  • Every interaction with clients reflects Islamic character (akhlaq)

Our Accountability

How we hold ourselves to this.

Standards without accountability are just words. We have built specific mechanisms to ensure these principles are not just declared — but practiced.

We invite the Muslim community to hold us to account. If you observe us violating any of these principles — in our marketing, our contracts, or our conduct — we want to know. Reach out directly.

Our accountability structure

Three practices we maintain to keep ourselves honest before Allah and the Ummah.

  • 1.Annual public review of these standards — we update them as our understanding of fiqh al-muamalat deepens
  • 2.Seeking guidance from Islamic scholars on novel marketing questions (AI, data privacy, digital contracts)
  • 3.Open community feedback — any Muslim who sees us fall short of these standards may contact us directly at standards@halalgrowthco.com
  • 4.Internal niyyah check before every client engagement — we begin with Bismillah and sincere intention
